AI Research LLM Evaluation AfterQuery

AI Reasoning
Benchmark

A novel multimodal, web-grounded reasoning benchmark for evaluating frontier AI models on human-like complex search tasks — built from scratch.

Type
Original research
Timeline
2025 — 2026
Domain
LLM eval · AI research
Output
Benchmark + eval infrastructure
Context

Existing benchmarks don't capture how humans actually search.

The lab builds human-generated datasets to evaluate how frontier AI models reason. Existing benchmarks test isolated knowledge. Real web tasks require navigating ambiguity, synthesizing across multiple sources, and handling multimodal information simultaneously.

My role was to develop a benchmark that captured this complexity: a task set that could accurately differentiate frontier LLMs on human-like search behavior that existing evals miss entirely.

Real search is ambiguous, multimodal, and recursive — the benchmark had to be the same.

Design Principle · 2026
Approach

Strategy-first, then infrastructure.

I started with the core strategic question: what does "human-like complex search" actually look like? I mapped the taxonomy of real tasks — cross-source synthesis, visual + text reasoning, ambiguous queries with multiple valid interpretations — and designed the benchmark structure around this reality.

From there, I built the evaluation infrastructure from scratch: task runners, model harnesses, a rubric-aligned scoring system, and analysis tooling to surface where models systematically fail.

The resulting benchmark surfaces meaningful differentiation between frontier models that existing evals flatten into noise — providing a defensible, human-grounded signal.
